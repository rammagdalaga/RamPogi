Questions(Wala malagay ah)

A push-up is a common calisthenics exercise performed in a prone position by raising and lowering the body using the arms. It primarily works the chest, shoulders, and triceps muscles.

A diamond push-up is a variation where you form a diamond shape with your hands. This targets the triceps more intensively than regular push-ups and also engages the inner chest muscles.

Close grip push-ups are performed with hands placed closer together than shoulder-width apart. This variation emphasizes the triceps and inner chest muscles more than standard push-ups.

For beginners, start with 2-3 sets of 8-12 repetitions. As you get stronger, you can increase to 3-4 sets of 15-20 repetitions. Always listen to your body and progress gradually.
